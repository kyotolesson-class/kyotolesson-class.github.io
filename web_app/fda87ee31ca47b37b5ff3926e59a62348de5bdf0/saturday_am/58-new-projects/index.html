August 21st, 2021

The students who are improving their typing speeds are enjoying some fun games that are bundled in with the Typing.com curriculum, so much so that these students decided they will make their own typing game as their next class project.

Since most students are starting new projects, I made a 'New Project Proposal' worksheet for students to draw mockups and write features of their website or game.

In the example below, the student is planning a typing game where correct presses on the keyboard improves the appearance of a character's face.



At the end of class, we played a cooperative game called “Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes.” In this game, one person is tasked with defusing a bomb, while everyone else has to communicate the defusing instructions. The person with the bomb can’t see the instructions, while the people with the instructions can’t see the bomb. Success in this game requires teamwork and excellent communication to solve challenging puzzles.
