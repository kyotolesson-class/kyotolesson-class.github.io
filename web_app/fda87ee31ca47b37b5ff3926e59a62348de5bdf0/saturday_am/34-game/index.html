Saturday February 13th, 2021

This week, I encouraged the kids to continue working on their projects from last week. Either this meant creating your own custom character in Figma, or if they were finished with that, inserting that character into an HTML game world, where the interaction is determined by JavaScript.

Most kids enjoyed the idea of inserting one character's head on another character's body. This is actually a slightly challenging thing to do in Figma, so I decided to list the steps here, for future reference:

  1. Choose your characters. Here, we'll put Sonic's head on Mario's body.
  2. Click on the pen tool.
  3. Slowly outline the target character's head with the pen tool. Be patient!
  4. After connecting all the dots, press "Enter" or "Return" to turn the outline into a smooth vector.
  5. While the vector is selected, click on the + button in the "Fill" section.
  6. The target character's head will fill itself with a grey color.
  7. Right click in the grey space and choose "Use as mask."
  8. In the layers column, move the target character image ABOVE the mask layer.
  9. You now have the head isolated!
  10. Now, simply move the head over the other character's body! Voila!
